The value of a journal is not in the writing but in the re-reading.

You write to offload — to move the thought from head to page so you stop carrying it. But the pages accumulate, and in six months you find a version of yourself you had forgotten. That person worried about things that resolved themselves. Had ideas they never pursued. Saw things clearly that are now muddled.

The journal as a practice is really a practice in longitudinal self-observation. See also [[on attention]] for thoughts on the underlying mechanism.
