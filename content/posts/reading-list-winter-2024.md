Books finished this quarter, briefly annotated.

**The Rings of Saturn** — Sebald. A walk through Suffolk that becomes a meditation on entropy, empire, and the persistence of grief. The longest sentences I have read and the most patient.

**How to Live** — Bakewell on Montaigne. The best argument I have encountered for the essay as a form. Montaigne was doing something structurally similar to [[on keeping a journal]] — using writing as a tool of self-observation rather than self-presentation.

**Obliquity** — Kay. Short, usefully contrarian. The central claim: most goals are achieved better by not pursuing them directly. The evidence is more suggestive than conclusive but the idea is worth sitting with.

Currently reading: *The Glass Bead Game*, Hesse.
