Personal sites used to be normal. You had a place on the web that was yours: idiosyncratic, hand-built, slow to update. Then platforms offered reach and convenience and most people handed both over.

I kept coming back to the same problem with existing static site generators: they solve for blogs or for wikis, not for both together. But the way I actually think is non-linear — a journal entry gestures at a concept, the concept lives in a wiki page, the wiki page links back to two other entries.

Folio is an attempt to build the simplest possible tool that lets those connections exist. See [[getting started]] for how to set it up, and [[on keeping a journal]] for why you might want to.

The design is deliberately influenced by [[colophon|this site's colophon]] and the aesthetics of [[xxiivv]] — monochrome, text-forward, no tracking, no ads, fast.
