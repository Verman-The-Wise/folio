Attention is the one resource that can't be replenished by working harder.

Sleep recovers energy. Rest recovers motivation. But attention — the capacity to hold something in focus long enough to actually think about it — is restored only by genuine idleness, by boredom, by the kind of unfocused wandering that most productivity systems are designed to eliminate.

This connects directly to [[on keeping a journal]]: the reason journaling works is that writing forces sustained attention on a single thread. You cannot skim your own half-formed thought.
